# Pathology Informatics Lectures — Viewing Guide

Welcome! These lectures are built as interactive web presentations. Below is a quick guide to help you navigate them before we meet.

---

## Getting Started

1. Open the lecture link in your browser — you will land on a **lecture selector** page listing all available presentations
2. Click the lecture you want to view
3. On the presenter configuration screen, click **"Skip and present anonymously"** to proceed directly to the slides
4. You are now in the presentation and can flip through slides at your own pace

## Navigation

- Use the **arrow keys** or **click the edges of the screen** to move between slides
- Some slides move **down** as well as right — follow the arrows
- At the bottom of the browser window you will find a **navigation dock** — a row of round icons that lets you jump directly to any slide
- The slide content is best viewed on a laptop or desktop screen

## Glossary

A **glossary of key terms** is accessible from the first and last slides of the lecture, and remains available throughout. If you see a term with a **dotted underline**, hover your mouse over it — a definition will appear. This is there so you are not lost with unfamiliar terminology.

## Exploring Further

After clicking "Skip and present anonymously," you will see a link to the **GitHub repository**. Feel free to explore the design and structure of the lecture if you are curious about how it was built.

## Before the Session

- Each lecture is interactive and designed for live discussion — viewing the slides on your own gives you the structure, but the real value is in the commentary and conversation during our session
- If anything in the slides sparks a question, **send it to me ahead of time** so we can address it during the lecture
- Content may be updated before the session, but the overall structure is set

Looking forward to a good session.
